# Skill 07 — Progressive Complications Audit

## Purpose
Progressive complications are the events between the inciting incident and the
crisis that force the protagonist into increasingly difficult situations. The key
word is PROGRESSIVE. Each complication must require the protagonist to risk more,
push harder, and pass a point of no return. Complications that repeat the same
level of difficulty are not progressive — they are the same event happening twice.

A flat middle section is almost always a failure of progressive complications.

## Inputs
- script.txt
- beat_sheet.txt
- 02_controlling_idea.md
- 05_act_structure.md

## Instructions

If a required input file is missing or does not contain the expected content, halt immediately and report the missing input to the orchestrator — do not proceed with partial context.

### Step 1 — List Every Major Complication
Working through the script, list every significant obstacle or complication
the protagonist faces between the inciting incident and the crisis. For each, note whether it escalates specifically toward the controlling idea's climactic argument — not just generically raising stakes, but tightening the vise on the story's central value conflict.
For each: one sentence describing what it is and what it costs the protagonist.

### Step 2 — Test for Escalation
For each complication (after the first), ask: does this require the protagonist
to risk MORE than the previous complication? Is the force opposing them stronger?
Mark each as ESCALATES / REPEATS / DECREASES.

### Step 3 — Check for Points of No Return
After each major complication: can the protagonist turn back and return to
who they were before? If yes at every stage, the complications have no teeth.
Mark each complication: POINT OF NO RETURN / REVERSIBLE.

### Step 4 — Check Strategy Variation
Is the protagonist being forced to CHANGE their approach as complications escalate?
Or are they using the same strategy repeatedly? Same strategy = stalled escalation,
even if the obstacles are technically getting bigger.

### Step 5 — Identify the Midpoint Complication
Is there a complication at roughly the midpoint that significantly raises the stakes
and forces the protagonist to change strategy? For a short film, this may be a
single moment — but it should be identifiable.

### Step 6 — Check for Filler
Is there any complication that could be removed without affecting the next one?
If yes, mark it as filler.

## Output Format

If the script does not provide enough material to complete this analysis confidently, mark the output [INCONCLUSIVE] at the top of the file, describe what is missing or ambiguous, and flag it for human review before the pipeline continues.

Save as `07_complications.md`:

```
COMPLICATIONS LIST:
1. [Description] — Escalates/Repeats/Decreases — Point of no return/Reversible
2. [etc.]

ESCALATION ASSESSMENT: [overall pattern — is it genuinely building?]
MIDPOINT COMPLICATION: [identified / not present]
STRATEGY VARIATION: [is the protagonist being forced to adapt?]
FILLER: [any complications that don't earn their place]

PROBLEMS: [specific, with scene references]
SUGGESTIONS: [concrete — what would make the weakest complication earn its place]
TEACHING MOMENT: [why "more of the same" is not escalation — what escalation actually means]
```
