# Skill 18 — Text and Subtext

## Purpose

Every great scene operates on two levels at once: the TEXT (what is literally happening — stated action, spoken words, visible behavior) and the SUBTEXT (what is actually happening beneath — what characters truly want, fear, mean). A scene with only text is shallow; a scene with only subtext is obscure. The skill is writing text that makes the subtext legible without stating it.

Unlike the dialogue-only exercises, this audits ALL layers: action, behavior, setting, objects, silence, AND dialogue.

## Inputs

- `script.txt`
- `15_scene_values.md`
- `17_gap.md`

## Instructions

### Step 1 — State text and subtext per key scene
Key = scenes carrying significant dramatic or thematic weight. One sentence each:
- **TEXT** — what literally happens
- **SUBTEXT** — what's actually happening beneath
If you can't write the subtext sentence, the scene may not have one — flag it.

### Step 2 — Audit each layer per scene
- **Dialogue** — surface topic; subtext sensable; any line stating subtext directly (on the nose)?
- **Action and behavior** — what physically happening? Carries subtext independently of dialogue?
- **Setting and objects** — does location/object carry thematic or subtext weight, or neutral?
- **Silence and gaps** — where do characters NOT speak when they could? Is silence working?

### Step 3 — Balance test
Mentally remove all dialogue. Subtext still sensable from behavior/setting/action? Yes = well-layered. No = too dependent on speech.

Mentally remove action and setting. Dialogue carries the subtext alone? Yes = other layers not contributing.

### Step 4 — Subtext surface moment
The moment in each scene where text and subtext are closest. Should be at the turning point. Usually.

## Output

Save as `18_text_subtext.md`. For each key scene:

- **TEXT** (one sentence) and **SUBTEXT** (one sentence) — or ABSENT
- **LAYER AUDIT** — Dialogue / Action / Setting / Silence — Layered or Not Contributing, with specific
- **BALANCE TEST** — without dialogue subtext survives Y/N; without action/setting dialogue carries it Y/N
- **SUBTEXT SURFACE MOMENT** — beat where text and subtext are closest

Then overall:

- **SCENES WITH FULL LAYERING** — count
- **SCENES TEXT-ONLY** (no subtext) — list
- **SCENES WHERE SUBTEXT IS PRESENT BUT INVISIBLE** — list
- **PROBLEMS** — scene-specific with layer references
- **SUGGESTIONS** — concrete (e.g., "Sc 5's alley sequence has strong subtext in dialogue but Florence's body is descriptively neutral. Her body should do something that contradicts or deepens what she's saying — even something small.")
- **TEACHING MOMENT** — 4-part structure; why scenes that make audience feel they're perceiving something unspoken are remembered
