# Skill 23 — Trialogue Technique

## Purpose

A *trialogue* is a scene where two characters use a THIRD ELEMENT — an object, topic, person, or activity — as the arena for a conflict they cannot address directly. Instead of fighting about the real subject, they fight about the canaries. The third element carries the subtext so the characters don't have to.

Both diagnostic and rewrite tool. Diagnoses scenes of emotional confrontation that are failing because characters speak too directly — and provides a structural solution.

The greater the distance between the third element and the true subject, the stronger the subtext.

## Inputs

- `script.txt`
- `20_on_the_nose.md`
- `22_said_unsaid.md`

## Instructions

### Step 1 — Identify candidate scenes
Using the on-the-nose diagnosis and inner-life map: scenes where two characters are in genuine emotional conflict but current dialogue is too direct or too vague. Candidates for trialogue.

### Step 2 — Name the true subject
For each candidate: what is the scene actually about? Real emotional conflict, real power dynamic, real fear or desire at the center.

### Step 3 — Find a third element
What object, topic, activity, or absent third party could both characters have a relationship to — and could serve as the displacement target?

Best third elements:
- Both characters can talk about it naturally without forcing
- Has qualities that MIRROR or CONTRAST the real subject
- Using it as proxy produces irony, tension, or humor

### Step 4 — Test the trialogue
Sketch how the scene would work if both characters spoke only about the third element. Does the real conflict come through? If you removed the third element and had them speak directly, would the scene be MORE or LESS powerful? More = trialogue isn't working. Less = it's working.

### Step 5 — Assess the existing script
Does the script already use trialogue successfully? Identify instances where a third element is already carrying subtext well, and where one could be introduced.

## Output

Save as `23_trialogue.md`:

- **EXISTING TRIALOGUE USE** — scenes already doing this well

For each candidate:
- **True subject** — real emotional conflict
- **Current problem** — too direct / not landing / on the nose
- **Proposed third element** — object/topic/person + why it works
- **Mirror/contrast quality** — how it reflects the real subject
- **Trialogue test** — does the real conflict survive the displacement?

Then:

- **STRONGEST OPPORTUNITY** — scene that would benefit most
- **PROBLEMS** — scenes where direct speech is standing in for finding a displacement target
- **SUGGESTIONS** — concrete third elements grounded in this script's world
- **TEACHING MOMENT** — 4-part structure; trialogue is not evasion but accurate picture of conflict; people in real conflict almost never address the real subject directly
