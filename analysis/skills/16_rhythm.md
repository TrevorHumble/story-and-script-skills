# Skill 16 — Rhythm and Pacing

## Purpose

*Rhythm* is the pattern of variation in scene length across a story. *Tempo* is the level of activity within each scene. A story that holds the same register — same length, same intensity, same emotional pitch — for too long becomes numbing even when individual scenes are strong. The audience needs contrast and recovery to feel each impact fully.

McKee: "Repetitiveness is the enemy of rhythm. When emotional experience repeats, the power of the second event is cut in half."

## Inputs

- `script.txt`
- `beat_sheet.txt`
- `05_act_structure.md`
- `15_scene_values.md`

## Instructions

### Step 1 — Map scene length and register
For each scene: approximate length (short / medium / long, relative to others) and dominant register (high tension, low tension, release, reflection, transition).

### Step 2 — Read the pattern
Look at length + register sequence as a whole. Measure rhythm against act structure. Find:
- Runs of 3+ scenes at the same register → flat passages
- Equal-length scenes running consecutively → rhythmically monotonous

### Step 3 — Test impact scenes
For each high-tension scene or major reversal: what immediately precedes it? Is there a lower-tension scene giving room to breathe before impact? A climax preceded by equal-intensity scenes will not land at full force.

### Step 4 — Tempo variation
Are all scenes running at the same activity level — all dialogue, all action, all visual? Vary method alongside length.

### Step 5 — Longest scene test
Identify the longest scene. Does it earn its length? A long scene that doesn't carry more weight is simply slow.

### Step 6 — Scene value rhythm
Using the chart from Skill 15: are consecutive scenes charging the same value polarity? Two consecutive positive-to-negative turns without relief may be numbing even if both work individually.

## Output

Save as `16_rhythm.md`:

- **RHYTHM MAP** — table: scene, length, register, value direction
- **FLAT PASSAGES** — consecutive same-register runs, listed
- **IMPACT SCENE BREATHING ROOM** — does each major reversal have recovery before it?
- **TEMPO VARIATION** — PASS/FAIL with explanation
- **LONGEST SCENE JUSTIFIED** — PASS/FAIL with which scene
- **VALUE RHYTHM** — are directions varying enough?
- **PROBLEMS** — scene-specific
- **SUGGESTIONS** — concrete (e.g., "Sc 4 and 5 are both high-tension. Brief scene of release between them — even 30 sec — would let the audience reset before Sc 5 hits.")
- **TEACHING MOMENT** — 4-part structure; why contrast is required, not luxury; rest is not weakness
