# Skill 07 — Progressive Complications

## Purpose

Progressive complications are the events between the inciting incident and the crisis that force the protagonist into increasingly difficult situations. The key word is *progressive*. Each complication must require the protagonist to risk more, push harder, pass a point of no return.

Complications that repeat the same level of difficulty are not progressive — they are the same event happening twice. A flat middle is almost always a failure of progressive complications.

## Inputs

- `script.txt`
- `beat_sheet.txt`
- `02_controlling_idea.md`
- `05_act_structure.md`

## Instructions

### Step 1 — List every major complication
Every significant obstacle between the inciting incident and the crisis. One sentence each: what it is, what it costs the protagonist. Note whether each escalates the controlling idea's central value conflict (not just generically raising stakes).

### Step 2 — Test for escalation
Each complication after the first: ESCALATES / REPEATS / DECREASES. Does it require more risk? Is the opposing force stronger?

### Step 3 — Points of no return
After each complication: can the protagonist turn back? POINT OF NO RETURN / REVERSIBLE.

### Step 4 — Strategy variation
Is the protagonist forced to *change approach* as complications escalate? Same strategy → stalled escalation, even if obstacles technically grow.

### Step 5 — Midpoint complication
Is there a complication around the midpoint that significantly raises stakes and forces strategy change? In a short, this may be a single moment.

### Step 6 — Filler check
Any complication that could be removed without affecting the next? Mark as filler.

## Output

Save as `07_complications.md`:

- **COMPLICATIONS LIST** — numbered: description, escalates/repeats/decreases, point of no return/reversible
- **ESCALATION ASSESSMENT** — overall pattern
- **MIDPOINT COMPLICATION** — identified or absent
- **STRATEGY VARIATION** — is protagonist adapting?
- **FILLER** — anything that doesn't earn its place
- **PROBLEMS** — scene-specific
- **SUGGESTIONS** — concrete
- **TEACHING MOMENT** — 4-part structure; why "more of the same" is not escalation
