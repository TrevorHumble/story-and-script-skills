# Skill 21 — Exposition as Ammunition

## Purpose

Exposition — backstory, context, worldbuilding, relationship history — is not the enemy. Exposition delivered AS EXPLANATION is. McKee's technique: have characters use information about the past as ammunition in their current conflicts. When a character reveals something because they NEED IT TO WIN RIGHT NOW, the exposition disappears into the action and the audience absorbs it without noticing.

McKee's paradigm: Darth Vader's "I am your father" lands at the moment of maximum conflict — as a weapon to disarm his son. Not as backstory. As ammunition.

## Inputs

- `script.txt`
- `19_beat_analysis.md`
- `20_on_the_nose.md`

## Instructions

### Step 1 — Inventory all exposition
Mark every passage of exposition: backstory, world explanation, relationship history, context-setting. Both dialogue and narrative description.

### Step 2 — Test each passage
Is this delivered because a character needs it as a weapon or tool in the current conflict — or because the audience needs to know it? If for audience: must be moved or transformed.

### Step 3 — Apply the withholding principle
Withhold exposition unless absolutely necessary for comprehension. Trivial facts come early. Critical secrets come last. Is the script revealing things in the right order, or giving away information that would be more powerful held until later?

### Step 4 — Find the ammunition moment
For each passage that needs to move: identify a moment of conflict elsewhere where this information would function as ammunition — where a character would USE this knowledge to get what they want right now.

### Step 5 — Check As-you-know-Bob lines
Cross-reference Skill 20's exposition problems. Characters still telling each other what they both already know? Resolve.

## Output

Save as `21_exposition.md`. For each passage:

- Scene + passage
- **Delivered for** — Audience / Character need
- **Works as ammunition** — Y/N
- **If no, suggested relocation** — where in the script this could become ammunition
- **Withholding issue** — revealed too early? Y/N

Then overall:

- **EXPOSITION HANDLED WELL** — count + working instances
- **EXPOSITION THAT STOPS THE STORY** — count + most urgent fixes
- **BEST AMMUNITION USE** — best example + why
- **PROBLEMS** — numbered, scene-specific
- **SUGGESTIONS** — concrete relocations
- **TEACHING MOMENT** — 4-part structure; exposition-as-explanation breaks the spell; best exposition is invisible
