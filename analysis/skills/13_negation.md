# Skill 13 — Negation of the Negation

## Purpose

McKee's most demanding thematic concept: the *negation of the negation* is not simply the opposite of the story's core value — it is that value in its most corrupt form, a negative disguised as something good. It is one thing to be imprisoned. It is another to be imprisoned by someone who claims to love you.

Stories that move only between positive and negative operate in a limited range. The most powerful stories descend into the negation of the negation — where the worst thing is not simply bad but is a corruption of something good — before the climax resolves or confirms that descent.

## Inputs

- `script.txt`
- `02_controlling_idea.md`
- `05_act_structure.md`

## Instructions

### Step 1 — Map the thematic square
Using the controlling idea's core value:

- **Positive** — the value fully achieved
- **Contrary** — absence of the value
- **Contradictory** — the direct opposite
- **Negation of the Negation** — the value corrupted; negative disguised as positive

Example with AUTONOMY:
- Positive: genuine self-determination
- Contrary: passivity / drifting
- Contradictory: control by others
- Negation of the negation: believing you're free while actively dismantling yourself

### Step 2 — Map the story's progression
Per act (or section in a short): where does the protagonist's situation fall on the square?

### Step 3 — Test for depth
Does the story descend into the negation of the negation before the climax? If not: is the script pulling its punches? Avoiding the most painful version of its own territory?

### Step 4 — Test the climax
Does the climax resolve the negation of the negation — by achieving the positive, or by confirming the tragic full descent? A climax that stops at the contradictory (simply bad) without reaching the negation (corrupted bad) may not have gone far enough.

### Step 5 — Other characters
Do any supporting characters embody the negation as a permanent state? Antagonists often live there.

## Output

Save as `13_negation.md`:

- **CORE VALUE** (from controlling idea)
- **THEMATIC SQUARE** — the four positions, specific to this story
- **STORY PROGRESSION** — position on square per act/section, plus climax
- **DEPTH TEST** — does story reach the negation? YES/NO/PARTIAL
- **CLIMAX TEST** — does it resolve from that deepest position?
- **CHARACTERS IN THE NEGATION**
- **PROBLEMS** — scene-specific
- **SUGGESTIONS** — concrete (how a specific scene could push into the negation)
- **TEACHING MOMENT** — 4-part structure; why corrupted-good hurts more than simply-bad
