# Skill 06 — Inciting Incident

## Purpose

The inciting incident is not just the first event — it is the event that changes everything. It must radically upset the balance of the protagonist's life, create a desire they must now pursue, and generate a question the audience cannot stop wondering about until the climax answers it.

A weak or delayed inciting incident is one of the most common structural failures. The story has nowhere to go if it doesn't know what's launched it.

## Inputs

- `script.txt`
- `beat_sheet.txt`
- `02_controlling_idea.md`
- `05_act_structure.md`

## Instructions

### Step 1 — Identify the event
Name the specific event. Not a mood shift — a concrete, dramatizable occurrence. Cross-check against the controlling idea: does this event force the protagonist into the story's central value conflict?

### Step 2 — Test for radical upset
Could the protagonist reasonably ignore this event and go back to normal life? If yes, the inciting incident is too weak. Moderate upset → moderate story.

### Step 3 — Test for desire
Does the event cause the protagonist to form a desire (conscious or unconscious) they must pursue? Does it match the want from Skill 03?

### Step 4 — Test for story question
Write the question the audience will be asking until the climax. Compelling enough to sustain the story?

### Step 5 — Check placement
For a short film, the inciting incident should arrive early — often in the first scene. If delayed, what is the story doing before? Is that pre-incident material earning its place?

### Step 6 — Check causality
Caused by something dramatizable in the story's world, or just an internal mood change? The inciting incident must happen *in the world*.

## Output

Save as `06_inciting_incident.md`:

- **INCITING INCIDENT** — the specific event, location in script
- **TESTS** — pass/fail for: radical upset, creates desire, matches spine, generates story question, placement, causality
- **PROBLEMS** — scene-specific
- **SUGGESTIONS** — concrete
- **TEACHING MOMENT** — 4-part structure; why upset must be RADICAL not moderate; why placement matters; why event must be dramatizable
