# Skill 05 — Act Structure

## Purpose

McKee's structural model: beats build scenes, scenes build sequences, sequences build acts, acts build the story. Each level climaxes in a more significant value change than the one below it. This skill maps the architecture and tests whether each structural beat is earning its turn.

For a short film the structure compresses but the rules apply. A short that skips its crisis or buries its inciting incident fails for the same reasons a feature does — just faster.

## Inputs

- `script.txt`
- `beat_sheet.txt`
- `02_controlling_idea.md`
- `03_story_spine.md`

## Instructions

### Step 1 — Locate and label structural beats
- **Inciting incident**: the event that radically upsets the protagonist's life
- **Act One climax**: the reversal that locks in central conflict
- **Midpoint** (if present): complication or revelation that raises stakes
- **Act Two climax**: forces protagonist to point of no return
- **Crisis**: the irreconcilable choice
- **Climax**: the final irreversible change
- **Resolution**: what follows

In a short, beats may compress into the same moment — note when.

### Step 2 — Test each beat
- Actually present, or implied/skipped?
- Decisive value reversal — more extreme than anything within its act?
- Irreversible? Can the story go back?

### Step 3 — Proportions
Does each act feel proportionally weighted? Is one section carrying too much while another rushes? Even a two-minute drag in a short is a problem.

### Step 4 — Spine through structure
Using the spine: is the want driving each beat? Does the inciting incident establish the want? Does the climax resolve it?

### Step 5 — Climax against controlling idea
The climax must prove the controlling idea. If what happens at the climax does not demonstrate the argument — either the climax is wrong or the controlling idea needs revision.

## Output

Save as `05_act_structure.md`:

- **STRUCTURAL MAP** — each beat with scene/moment + value it upsets
- **BEAT TESTS** — pass/fail per beat for present, decisive, irreversible
- **PROPORTION ASSESSMENT** — heavy/light sections, why it matters
- **SPINE THROUGH STRUCTURE**
- **CLIMAX VS. CONTROLLING IDEA** — pass/fail with explanation
- **PROBLEMS** — missing/weak/misplaced beats, scene-specific
- **SUGGESTIONS** — concrete (e.g., "Crisis doesn't register as a dilemma because X. To earn it, protagonist needs to face choice between Y and Z in Scene 5.")
- **TEACHING MOMENT** — 4-part structure; why each beat must be more extreme than what's inside it; why climax-proves-controlling-idea is the test
