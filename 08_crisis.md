# Skill 08 — Crisis

## Purpose

The crisis is the story's moment of supreme decision. McKee is precise: a TRUE DILEMMA — a choice between irreconcilable goods, or the lesser of two evils — under the maximum pressure of the protagonist's life. A *static* moment: the audience held in the pain of the impossible choice before the climactic action releases them.

A weak crisis produces an unearned climax. If the protagonist never truly suffers the weight of an impossible choice, the resolution feels cheap.

## Inputs

- `script.txt`
- `02_controlling_idea.md`
- `05_act_structure.md`
- `07_complications.md`

## Instructions

### Step 1 — Locate the crisis
Find the moment of supreme decision immediately before the climactic action. The crisis dilemma must directly test the value the controlling idea names — it is the controlling idea on trial.

### Step 2 — Name the two choices
Both options, explicit, specific to *this* story and *this* character. Not "love or duty," but "give Sebastian the last gum, or keep it and escape."

### Step 3 — Test for genuine dilemma
Best Bad Choice (two bad options) or Irreconcilable Goods (two good options the protagonist can't have both)? If one option is clearly better with no real cost, it is NOT a genuine dilemma. Test: does the protagonist suffer choosing? Does the audience?

### Step 4 — Maximum pressure
Is this the moment of most extreme pressure in the protagonist's life in this story? If they've been in worse situations earlier, the crisis is misplaced.

### Step 5 — Static quality
Does the story pause at this moment? Or does the protagonist decide too quickly, before the audience feels the weight? The audience must be held in the suffering before the climactic action releases them.

### Step 6 — Irreversibility
After the choice, can it be undone? The climax that follows must be irreversible.

## Output

Save as `08_crisis.md`:

- **CRISIS LOCATION**, **CHOICE A**, **CHOICE B** (each with specific cost)
- **DILEMMA TYPE** — Best Bad Choice / Irreconcilable Goods / NOT A GENUINE DILEMMA
- **TESTS** — pass/fail for: genuine dilemma, maximum pressure, static quality, irreversibility
- **PROBLEMS** — scene-specific
- **SUGGESTIONS** — concrete
- **TEACHING MOMENT** — 4-part structure; decision vs. dilemma; why the crisis is load-bearing for the climax
