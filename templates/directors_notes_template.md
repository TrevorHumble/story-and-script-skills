## How this document works

**This document describes WHAT the staging IS, not WHAT IT IS MEANT TO ACHIEVE.** Agents read the script and the staging here, then infer intent from the work itself. Pre-loading intent (e.g., "this is meant to feel intimate") corrupts analysis — the agent will then find what it was told to find rather than what is actually on the page.

**Show, don't tell. Mechanically describe the staging.** No value words ("powerful," "intimate," "rich"). No effect framing ("this is meant to..."). The staging IS the intent; if it isn't legible from the staging alone, the staging needs to change.

**Every revision section cites its source.** Either a skill output (e.g., "Source: 10_true_character.md, Problem 2") or "developed in conversation following [skill] pit stop." This lets future-you trace why a decision was made.
