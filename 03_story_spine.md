# Skill 03 — Story Spine

## Purpose

The story spine is the through-line of desire that holds the whole story together: the protagonist's conscious *want* and unconscious *need*, established by the inciting incident and resolved only at the climax. If the spine is unclear or shifts without dramatic cause, the story feels directionless even when individual scenes work.

A *want* is what the protagonist consciously chases. A *need* is what they actually require — often in direct contradiction to the want. The most resonant stories make the protagonist choose between them.

## Inputs

- `script.txt`
- `02_controlling_idea.md`

## Instructions

### Step 1 — State the want
A specific infinitive. Not "wants to be happy" (that's a mood). Specific enough to write a scene that advances or blocks it.

### Step 2 — State the need
Same form: specific infinitive. The thing that would genuinely change the protagonist's life, often the thing they're resisting.

### Step 3 — Spine integrity
- Inciting incident establishes the want?
- Climax resolves the want — by achieving it, failing, or consciously abandoning it for something more important?

### Step 4 — Trace through the middle
Act by act (or scene by scene for a short): is the want still the engine? Mark any moment where the spine drops or another desire takes over without dramatic cause.

### Step 5 — Want/need tension
Does pursuing the want make satisfying the need harder? The best stories make the surface goal the very thing standing between protagonist and what they actually need.

### Step 6 — Connect to controlling idea
The spine and the controlling idea should be in conversation. The want should lead toward (or away from) the value in the controlling idea.

## Output

Save as `03_story_spine.md`:

- **PROTAGONIST**, **WANT**, **NEED**, **WANT/NEED TENSION**
- **SPINE INTEGRITY** — pass/fail for inciting establishes want, want drives middle, climax resolves want, need is addressed by climax
- **CONNECTION TO CONTROLLING IDEA**
- **PROBLEMS** — scene-specific
- **SUGGESTIONS** — concrete
- **TEACHING MOMENT** — 4-part structure; want vs. need; why "wants to be happy" isn't a spine
